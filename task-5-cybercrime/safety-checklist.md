#  Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

##  Purpose
This checklist provides practical tips for college students in India to stay safe from cyber threats, scams, and online fraud.

---

# General Online Safety Tips

1.  Use strong and unique passwords  
   - Combine letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password everywhere.

2.  Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)  
   - Adds an extra layer of security to your accounts.

3.  Be careful with suspicious links and emails  
   - Do not click unknown links or download attachments from untrusted sources.

4.  Keep your devices updated  
   - Regular updates fix security vulnerabilities in apps and systems.

5.  Use secure Wi-Fi networks  
   - Avoid logging into important accounts on public Wi-Fi.

6.  Check website authenticity  
   - Ensure the URL starts with "https://" before entering personal data.

---

##  Financial / UPI Safety (Important)

7.  Never share your UPI PIN or OTP  
   - Banks or apps will NEVER ask for your PIN or OTP.

8.  Verify payment requests carefully  
   - Always check the receiver's name before approving any UPI transaction.

9.  Avoid QR code scams  
   - Scammers may send QR codes to receive money instead of sending it.

10.  Use official banking apps only  
   - Download apps only from Play Store/App Store.

---

##  Social Media & Personal Safety

11.  Limit personal information sharing  
   - Avoid posting phone numbers, addresses, or live location.

12.  Adjust privacy settings  
   - Control who can see your posts and personal details.

13.  Be aware of fake profiles  
   - Do not accept requests from unknown people.

---

##  If You Face Cyber Fraud

- Immediately report at: **https://cybercrime.gov.in**  
- Call helpline: **1930**  

Quick action can help recover lost money and prevent further damage.

---

## 💡 Conclusion
Staying safe online requires awareness and responsible behavior. By following these prevention tips, students can protect their data, finances, and digital identity from cyber threats.
