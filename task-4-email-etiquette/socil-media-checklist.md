#  Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist

##  Purpose
This checklist helps college students use social media responsibly, safely, and professionally in their daily lives.

---

##  DO's (Things You Should Do)

1.  Think before you post  
   - Always check if your content is appropriate and respectful before sharing.

2.  Maintain a professional profile  
   - Keep your bio, profile picture, and posts suitable for academic and career opportunities.

3.  Use strong privacy settings  
   - Control who can see your posts and personal information.

4.  Share useful and meaningful content  
   - Post educational, informative, or positive content that adds value.

5.  Respect others online  
   - Use polite language and respect different opinions.

6.  Verify information before sharing  
   - Always check the authenticity of news and posts to avoid spreading misinformation.

7.  Manage your time wisely  
   - Limit screen time and avoid excessive use of social media.

8.  Build a positive digital identity  
   - Use social media to showcase your skills, achievements, and talents.

---

##  DON'Ts (Things You Should Avoid)

1.  Don’t share personal information publicly  
   - Avoid posting phone numbers, addresses, or sensitive data.

2.  Don’t spread fake news or rumors  
   - Sharing unverified information can create confusion and harm others.

3.  Don’t post offensive or inappropriate content  
   - Avoid content that may hurt sentiments or damage your reputation.

4.  Don’t engage in online arguments  
   - Stay away from unnecessary debates, trolling, or negative discussions.

5.  Don’t use weak passwords  
   - Weak security can lead to hacking or data theft.

6.  Don’t overshare your personal life  
   - Keep a balance between privacy and sharing.

7.  Don’t trust unknown links or messages  
   - Avoid clicking suspicious links to protect your data.

8.  Don’t ignore digital footprint  
   - Remember, everything you post online can have long-term consequences.

---

##  Conclusion
Responsible use of social media helps in building a positive image, maintaining privacy, and ensuring safe online interactions. By following these do's and don'ts, students can use social media effectively for learning, networking, and personal growth.
